# IoT Device Pairing - Quick Start Guide

## 5-Minute Setup

### Step 1: Prepare Your Device
- Turn on your Bluetooth health device (smartwatch, fitness band, etc.)
- Put it in **discovery/pairing mode** (usually hold a button for 3-5 seconds)
- Make sure Bluetooth is **enabled** on your computer
- Keep device within **10 feet** of your computer

### Step 2: Start Pairing
1. Open your RemedoC patient dashboard
2. Look for **"IoT & Wearable Health Sync"** section
3. Click the **"+ Add Device"** button
4. This opens the device pairing modal

### Step 3: Scan for Devices
1. Click **"üì° Scan for Bluetooth Devices"** button
2. Your browser will ask for permission - click **"Allow"**
3. The system will scan for 5-10 seconds
4. Your device should appear in the list below

### Step 4: Connect Device
1. Look for your device in the **"Found Devices"** list
2. Click the **"Connect"** button next to it
3. Wait 2-3 seconds for connection to establish
4. You'll see a **success message** when connected
5. The modal will **auto-close** after 2 seconds

### Step 5: View Your Data
1. Your device now appears in **"Connected Devices"**
2. The system automatically syncs vitals **every 5 minutes**
3. Latest readings show in **"Latest Vitals"** cards
4. Historical data appears in **"Vitals History"** table

**That's it! Your device is now syncing.** ‚úÖ

## Troubleshooting

### ‚ùå "No Bluetooth support"
**Problem**: Browser doesn't support Bluetooth
**Solution**: Use Chrome, Edge, or Opera browser

### ‚ùå "No devices found"
**Problem**: Your device isn't appearing in scan
**Solutions**:
1. Make sure device is **powered on**
2. Put device in **pairing/discovery mode**
3. Move **closer** to computer
4. Check device **Bluetooth is discoverable**
5. Try **scanning again**

### ‚ùå "Permission denied"
**Problem**: You rejected browser Bluetooth access
**Solutions**:
1. **Reload the page**
2. Try clicking "Scan" again
3. Check browser permission settings
4. Try **incognito/private browsing**

### ‚ùå "Connection failed"
**Problem**: Device discovered but won't connect
**Solutions**:
1. **Restart your device** (power off/on)
2. Check device **battery level** (too low might fail)
3. Try **scanning again**
4. Move **closer** to computer
5. Try **different browser** (Chrome vs Edge)

### ‚ùå "Device disconnected during sync"
**Problem**: Connection lost while syncing
**Solutions**:
1. Move **closer** to computer
2. **Reconnect** the device
3. Check for **Bluetooth interference** (move away from microwaves, WiFi routers)
4. Update device **firmware** if available

### ‚ùå "Vitals not syncing"
**Problem**: Device connected but no data
**Solutions**:
1. Verify device still shows as **"Connected"**
2. Check device **battery level**
3. Wait for **next sync cycle** (5 minutes default)
4. Try **disconnecting and reconnecting**

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Use Chrome/Edge** for most reliable Bluetooth
2. **Keep device close** during initial connection
3. **Full battery** on both devices before syncing
4. **No interference** - avoid microwaves, cordless phones
5. **Sync regularly** - check vitals at least daily
6. **Update firmware** - keep device firmware current
7. **Allow permission** - always allow browser Bluetooth access
8. **One connection at a time** - don't pair while connected to other apps

## Vitals Explained

### ‚ù§Ô∏è Heart Rate (HR)
- Normal: 60-100 bpm at rest
- Higher when active/stressed
- Monitor trends over time

### üí® Oxygen Saturation (SpO‚ÇÇ)
- Normal: 95-100%

### üìä Blood Pressure (BP)
- Format: Systolic/Diastolic
- Normal: Under 120/80
- High: 140/90 or above
- Monitor for hypertension

### üå°Ô∏è Temperature
- Normal: 97-99¬∞F (36-37¬∞C)
- Over 100.4¬∞F = fever
- Under 95¬∞F = hypothermia

### ‚öñÔ∏è Weight
- Track trends weekly
- Daily weight varies 2-3 lbs
- Useful for health monitoring

### ü©∏ Glucose (Blood Sugar)
- Normal fasting: 70-100 mg/dL
- After meals: Under 140 mg/dL
- Critical if under 70 or over 250

## Advanced Settings

### Adjust Sync Interval
By default, vitals sync every 5 minutes. To change:
1. Click connected device
2. Change "Sync Interval" to desired minutes (5-60)
3. Longer intervals use less battery
4. Shorter intervals give more frequent updates

### View Sync History
1. Check "Last sync" time under device
2. Look at "Vitals History" table for all readings
3. Timestamps show when each reading was taken

### Monitor Battery
1. Device battery % shown under device name
2. Below 20% might affect connection quality
3. Charge device if battery low
